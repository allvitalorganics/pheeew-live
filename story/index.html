Before

The shelf is broken.

Capsules that need an empty stomach. Powders that need a blender. $14 bottles that need a fridge. "Routines" people quit by week three. The gut category sells aspiration. It rarely sells follow-through.

After

One pouch. Daily.

80mL. Hot-fill. Shelf-stable for 18 months. Pour-and-sip in 30 seconds. 10g of clinical fiber and 1 billion CFU of a probiotic strain that survives stomach acid, bile, and the heat of manufacturing.

Pheeew

The bridge.

We took the format people already finish — a smoothie — and put a serious dose inside it. The pouch is the point. It survives the habit.

What we won't compromise

Three rules. No exceptions.

01
Clinical dose, or it doesn't ship

If the studied dose for an active is 5g, we don't ship 1g and call it a serving. The dose is the product.

02
Real food, or it doesn't ship

Fruit puree, not flavor concentrate. No synthetic sweeteners. Sweetness comes from the fruit or it doesn't come.

03
A habit, or it doesn't ship

Anything that needs a fridge, a blender, or an empty stomach is not a habit. Pour-and-sip is the habit.

Naju, South Korea
Where it's made

Korea. A hot-fill line.

The pouch is produced in a FDA-registered, HACCP-certified, GMP food facility in Korea on a hot-fill puree line. Fruit goes in. Fiber and probiotic are added at the temperature window the spore-form probiotic survives. The pouch is filled and sealed in one continuous run.
