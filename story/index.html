How we got to v4.0

Twelve formulas.
Three ingredients.

We deleted more than we added. The version that ships is the leanest one that still works.

2025 · Q3

The complaint.

Both of us bought gut products in two countries and didn't trust either. The American ones were dosed for marketing. The Korean ones were dosed for regulatory minimums. None of them were dosed for results.

2025 · Q4

First conversations.

We brought a brief to manufacturers in Korea, India, and the US: smallest possible formula, real fiber dose, named strain, no sweeteners. Every conversation started with someone explaining why our brief wasn't possible.

2025 · Nov

Formula v1.0 — too ambitious.

11 ingredients, 7 functional claims, multiple flavor variants. Looked great on a deck. Wouldn't have shipped a single SKU we believed in.

2026 · Jan

v2.0 — starting to delete.

Dropped flavor masking. Dropped two adaptogens we couldn't dose meaningfully. Down to seven ingredients.

2026 · Feb

v3.0 — strain locked.

Switched probiotic to Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969 from Sanzyme. Spore-form gives shelf stability without the cold chain¹.

2026 · Mar

v3.5 — went unflavored, then re-flavored honestly.

Stripped flavor entirely to test pure dose. Tasted like fiber. Re-introduced peach purée only — no stevia, no monk fruit, no sucralose.

2026 · Apr

v4.0 — locked.

Three core ingredients: 8g Fibersol-2L (ADM), 2g XOS, 1×10⁹ live spore cultures of B. coagulans SNZ 1969 (Sanzyme). Plus filtered water, peach purée, citric acid, natural peach flavor, ascorbic acid as preservative. R&D submitted.

2026 · Q3

First sticks ship.

Founders' list opens at $29 trial pricing for the first 200 customers. Then standard ladder.

Where it's made

Real addresses.

Every ingredient comes from a named manufacturer. No proprietary blends to hide a sourcing chain.

Fiber base
ADM
Decatur, Illinois · USA

Fibersol-2L is a soluble corn fiber (resistant maltodextrin) made by Archer Daniels Midland. Non-GMO. The most extensively studied resistant maltodextrin in clinical literature².

Supplies: Fibersol-2L · 8g
Probiotic strain
Sanzyme
Hyderabad, India

Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969 spore preparation. FDA GRAS GRN 597 + 864¹ cover this exact strain. Sanzyme is the original source — no licensee chain.

Supplies: SNZ 1969 · 1×10⁹ CFU

The thesis

Lean portfolio.
Heavy lifters.

Pheeew is the first brand from Pheeew, Inc. — a Delaware C-Corp founded by Alex, Jason, and Dawon. The plan isn't to build twenty brands. It's to build a small number of brands that each earn their place.

3

Anchor ingredients

Every Pheeew product starts with Fibersol-2L, XOS, and SNZ 1969. New formulations add function-specific actives — never to fix the foundation, only to extend it.
