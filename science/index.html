What is a synbiotic? A synbiotic is a prebiotic and a probiotic in one product. The prebiotic is fiber that selectively feeds beneficial gut bacteria. The probiotic is a live bacterial strain. Pheeew is a complementary synbiotic per the ISAPP 2020 consensus (Swanson et al., PMID 32826966) — both ingredients earn their evidence independently before they're combined.

A definition we want to be precise about

Pheeew is a complementary synbiotic.

Two kinds of synbiotic. We're one of them.

The ISAPP (International Scientific Association for Probiotics and Prebiotics) drew a line in 2020. Synbiotics fall into two categories: synergistic (where the prebiotic only feeds the probiotic in the same product) and complementary (where the prebiotic and probiotic each have independent benefits and combine for additive effect).

Pheeew is the second kind. Fibersol-2L and XOS feed the bacteria already living in your colon — your existing microbiome — not just the spore we add. Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969 has its own evidence base for bloating and stool consistency, independent of the fiber. Together: additive. Not synergistic.

Reference: Swanson KS, et al. Nature Reviews Gastroenterology & Hepatology, 2020. PMID 32826966.

Strain identity

Why SNZ 1969 specifically.

"Bacillus coagulans" is a species. SNZ 1969 is a strain. Different strains within the same species can have meaningfully different clinical effects — strain identity matters.

FDA GRAS-affirmed twice

Sanzyme submitted SNZ 1969 to the FDA's Generally Recognized as Safe process and received affirmative no-objection letters under GRN 597 and GRN 864. These cover the exact preparation we use.

What that means: independent expert review concluded SNZ 1969 is safe for general food and supplement use at our dose level.

Spore-form ≠ generic probiotic

Most probiotics on shelf are vegetative (active form) Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium. They die in stomach acid unless protected by enteric coating, refrigeration, or fancy delivery systems.

Bacillus coagulans is naturally spore-forming. The spore is dormant — metabolically inactive, acid-resistant, heat-resistant. No protective system needed.

Heat-stable up to 180°F

Spore-form survives temperatures that destroy vegetative probiotics. You can stir Pheeew into hot coffee or tea without killing the probiotic.

This is also why we don't refrigerate. Refrigerated probiotics are a logistics tax — and they only need refrigeration because they were the wrong form factor in the first place.

Not the same as LactoSpore or GBI-30

Other Bacillus coagulans strains exist (LactoSpore MTCC 5856 by Sabinsa, GanedenBC30 GBI-30 6086 by Kerry). Each has its own evidence base.

We chose SNZ 1969 because the GRAS coverage is broader, the strain is matched in our specific clinical reference (Murthy 2025), and Sanzyme is the original source — no licensee chain.
