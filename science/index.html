Clinical evidence on SNZ 1969

Three studies. By name.

2022 · IBS

IBS efficacy RCT

N: 92 patients, IBS-C + IBS-D
Dose: 500M CFU twice daily
Duration: 60 days
Design: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
Result: 90% of IBS-C and 100% of IBS-D responded vs 0% placebo.

IJBCP ↗

2021 · Constipation

Transit time RCT

N: Healthy adults with mild constipation
Duration: 8 weeks
Design: Randomized, controlled
Outcome: Transit time + bowel movement quality
Result: Significant transit-time improvement and bowel-discomfort reduction vs placebo.

PubMed PMID 34119240 ↗

2024 · Immune

Immunomodulatory RCT

N: Healthy adults aged 60–65
Endpoint: NK cell activity
Design: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
Compliance: 100% · no SAEs
Result: NK cell activity +42.07% vs placebo.

PMC 12624130 ↗

References

Every claim. Primary source.

Pheeew is a conventional food product, not a supplement or a drug. References below describe published research on the ingredient strains and components — not health claims about Pheeew.

Prebiotic fiber · XOS

  1. 1. Childs CE, et al. (2014). Xylo-oligosaccharides alone or in synbiotic combination with Bifidobacterium animalis subsp. lactis induce bifidogenesis and modulate markers of immune function in healthy adults: a double-blind, placebo-controlled, randomised, factorial cross-over study. British Journal of Nutrition. Healthy adults (25–65y) given 8g/day XOS for 21 days showed measurable bifidogenesis and modulated immune markers. PubMed 24661576 ↗
  2. 2. Finegold SM, et al. (2014). Xylooligosaccharide increases bifidobacteria but not lactobacilli in human gut microbiota. Food & Function. Demonstrates selective bifidogenic effect of XOS at 1.4g and 2.8g daily doses over 8 weeks in healthy adults — well-tolerated, no GI side effects. PubMed 24513849 ↗

Prebiotic fiber · Fibersol-2®

  1. 3. Goda T, et al. (2006). The maximum single dose of resistant maltodextrin that does not cause diarrhea in humans. Journal of Nutritional Science and Vitaminology. Establishes safe-dose threshold of 1.0g/kg body weight (men) and 1.1g/kg (women) — multiples above our serving. PubMed 24064737 ↗
  2. 4. Ye Z, et al. (2015). Soluble dietary fiber (Fibersol-2) decreased hunger and increased satiety hormones in humans when ingested with a meal. Nutrition Research. Demonstrates Fibersol-2's effect on appetite regulation and post-meal satiety. PubMed 25823991 ↗

Fiber intake gap · public health context

  1. 5. Quagliani D, Felt-Gunderson P (2017). Closing America's Fiber Intake Gap. American Journal of Lifestyle Medicine. Confirms that less than 5% of US adults meet the FDA's 28g/day fiber target; median intake is 12–17g/day — a 40–60% shortfall. PMC 6124841 ↗

Probiotic · Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969®

  1. 6. Madempudi RS, et al. (2019). Efficacy and safety of Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969 in IBS: randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial. International Journal of Basic & Clinical Pharmacology. N=92; 500M CFU twice daily × 60 days. 90% of IBS-C and 100% of IBS-D responded vs 0% placebo. IJBCP ↗
  2. 7. Madempudi RS, et al. (2021). Spore-forming Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969 improved intestinal motility and constipation perception. Journal of Functional Foods. 8-week RCT in healthy adults with mild intermittent constipation — significant transit-time and bowel-comfort improvements vs placebo. PubMed 34119240 ↗
  3. 8. Madempudi RS, et al. (2024). Immunomodulatory effects of probiotic Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969 in healthy adults. RCT in adults 60–65y; NK cell activity +42.07% vs placebo. 100% compliance, no serious adverse events. PMC 12624130 ↗
  4. 9. FDA GRAS Notice (Bacillus coagulans). US FDA GRAS Inventory — Bacillus coagulans is recognized as safe for use in conventional food at the levels used in Pheeew. FDA GRAS Inventory ↗
