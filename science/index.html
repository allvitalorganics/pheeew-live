◆ The Science

Two actives.
Both earn their place.

Pheeew is a synbiotic — a prebiotic fiber blend and a probiotic strain working together. We pick ingredients that have been studied, GRAS-affirmed, and dosed at levels the research actually used. The rest is real fruit.

What "synbiotic" means

Prebiotic + probiotic. Not postbiotic.

Synbiotic is the formal term for a product that contains both a prebiotic (a fiber the good bacteria eat) and a probiotic (live good bacteria themselves). The prebiotic feeds the probiotic. The probiotic survives the journey better because the fiber is already there.

Postbiotic is a different category — the leftover metabolic byproducts of bacteria, sold separately. Pheeew is a synbiotic, not a postbiotic. We're clear about the difference because the supplement aisle isn't.

Why pair them

Probiotics alone work but die quickly without the right food in the gut.

Prebiotics alone feed whatever's already living there — which might or might not be what you want.

Synbiotic seeds and feeds at the same time. The 1B CFU strain has its own food show up with it.

The fiber · 10g per pouch

XOS + Fibersol-2. Two fibers with different jobs.

XOS
Xylo-oligosaccharide

A short-chain prebiotic fermented selectively by Bifidobacteria — the family of gut bugs most associated with the things people actually want (regularity, less bloat, better digestion). Studies show measurable Bifidobacteria population increases within 2–4 weeks of consistent intake.

XOS works at low doses (most studies use 1–4g/day). At our 10g combined blend we're well within the comfortable range.

Fibersol-2®
Resistant maltodextrin · Matsutani/ADM

A clinically-studied soluble fiber that adds bulk, supports regularity, and slows glucose absorption (which is why it shows up in functional foods globally). 90+ published studies on digestive comfort, bowel function, satiety, and post-meal glucose response.

Fibersol-2 is well-tolerated at higher doses — most products dose 5–10g/day. Ours sits inside that range.

What 10g of fiber actually means

The FDA's Daily Value for fiber is 28g per day. Most Americans hit 12–16g. A single Pheeew pouch delivers ~36% of the daily fiber target in 80mL — without making you chew through a stack of vegetables, choke down a powdered drink, or eat a bowl of cardboard cereal.

The probiotic · 1B CFU per pouch

Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969®

A spore-forming probiotic strain from Sanzyme Biologics. The "spore-form" part matters: it means the strain protects itself with a shell that survives stomach acid, bile, the 180°F hot-fill process that makes our pouch shelf-stable, and 18 months of ambient storage. The strain wakes up in the small intestine where it's needed, then does its job.

Spore-form, heat-stable

Survives the hot-fill manufacturing that makes Pheeew shelf-stable for 18 months at room temp. No refrigeration. No "live in transit" gambles. The CFUs you see on the label are the CFUs in the pouch on the day you drink it.

GRAS-affirmed

"Generally Recognized As Safe" status from the FDA for use in conventional food. That's why Pheeew is a food, not a supplement — the strain doesn't require the supplement category to be legal.

30+ clinical studies

Specifically on SNZ 1969. Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled studies on IBS, constipation transit time, and immune markers. We cite the three most relevant below.

Clinical evidence on SNZ 1969

Three studies we cite by name.

We don't make health or disease claims about Pheeew (Pheeew is a conventional food, not a supplement). These studies are presented as ingredient-level science on the probiotic strain we use, not as claims about what a pouch will do for you.

2022 · IBS

IBS efficacy study

Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled. 92 patients with IBS-C or IBS-D received 500M CFU of SNZ 1969 twice daily for 60 days. 90% of IBS-C and 100% of IBS-D patients responded versus 0% placebo. Significant reductions in abdominal pain, bloating, constipation, diarrhea.

International Journal of Basic & Clinical Pharmacology ↗

2021 · Constipation

Transit time RCT

Randomized controlled trial in healthy adults with mild intermittent constipation. 8 weeks of SNZ 1969 supplementation significantly improved colonic transit time, complete spontaneous bowel movement scores (weeks 2 and 9), and bowel discomfort symptoms (weeks 3 and 6) vs placebo.

PubMed PMID 34119240 ↗

2024 · Immune

Immunomodulatory trial

Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial in healthy adults aged 60–65. NK cell activity increased +42.07% versus placebo (net difference between groups). 100% compliance. No serious adverse events.

PMC PMCID 12624130 ↗

How it's made

Hot-fill puree, made in Korea.

The pouch is produced in a FDA-registered, HACCP-certified, GMP food facility in Korea. The line is a hot-fill process: real fruit puree is heated, the actives are added at the right temperature window for the spore-form probiotic to survive, then the pouch is filled and sealed in one continuous run. That's what gets us to 18 months of ambient shelf life with no refrigeration and no preservatives.

Every batch has a Certificate of Analysis covering microbial counts, CFU verification (we verify 1B CFU/pouch at production, not at theoretical formulation), fiber content, pH, and fill volume. The CoAs sit on file with the importer of record.

What we don't claim

Pheeew is a conventional food.

We don't claim Pheeew cures, treats, or prevents any disease. We don't claim it boosts immunity, fixes your skin, or unlocks energy. The studies above are about the probiotic strain at clinical doses, not about what a single pouch will do for any individual person.
