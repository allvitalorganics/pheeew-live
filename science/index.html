A definition we want to be precise about

Pheeew is a
complementary synbiotic.

The ISAPP (International Scientific Association for Probiotics and Prebiotics) drew a line in 2020. There are two kinds. We're one of them.

Prebiotic

The food. Fiber the resident gut microbes can ferment. We use 12g Fibersol-2L (long-chain) and 2g XOS (short-chain) for a fuller fermentation curve.

Probiotic

The live strain. We use a spore-form B. coagulans SNZ 1969 — robust enough to survive shelf, stomach, and bile, then activate in the gut.

Complementary synbiotic

Each component chosen on its own merits. Not synergistic — that would require evidence that this specific prebiotic preferentially feeds this probiotic. We don't claim that.

Reference: Swanson KS et al., ISAPP Consensus Statement on Synbiotics, Nat Rev Gastroenterol Hepatol, 2020 · PMID 32826966.

Where each part acts

Different jobs,
different stops.

The fiber and the spore work in different parts of the gut. Both end up in the colon, but they take different paths.

Two ingredients. Two pathways.

01

Stomach acid

Most probiotics die here. Spore-form SNZ 1969 stays dormant inside its protective spore coat — pH 1.5 doesn't break it.

02

Small intestine

Bile salts and pancreatic enzymes signal the spore to germinate. The active strain wakes up here.

03

Colon

Fibersol-2L and XOS finally meet your gut microbes. They get fermented into short-chain fatty acids, the actual fuel for the colon lining.

What we will & won't claim

The honest column
goes on the right.

We'd rather under-promise and have you re-order than ride a wave of language we can't defend. Here's the line, drawn out loud.

We can defend these

Backed by the formula and the literature.

  • +Provides 14g of dietary fiber per serving — meaningful contribution toward the 25–38g daily recommendation.
  • +Delivers 2 billion CFU of Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969, an FDA GRAS-recognized spore-form strain.
  • +Provides 14g of dietary fiber — recognized by US FDA Daily Values and Korean MFDS as a functional fiber dose.
  • +Resistant maltodextrin (Fibersol-2L) is clinically studied at 12g+ for bowel regularity and post-meal blood-sugar response.
  • +Spore-form probiotic — shelf-stable, survives stomach acid intact.
  • +Complementary synbiotic per the 2020 ISAPP consensus statement.
  • +Four ingredients — every gram on the label, no proprietary blends.
We won't claim these

Things you'll see other gut brands say. Not us.

  • —Not a treatment, cure, or prevention for any disease or condition.
  • —Lowers cholesterol or triglycerides — we deliberately under-dose for that claim.
  • —Not a weight-loss product. We don't promise inches, pounds, or "leaning out."
  • —Not an immune booster. The science there isn't where the marketing is.
  • —Not a "detox" or "cleanse." Your liver and kidneys handle that.
  • —Not a guaranteed bloating fix. Bodies are bodies; results vary.
  • —Not an energy stimulant. There's no caffeine and no adaptogen theater.
  • —Not a synergistic synbiotic — it's complementary, per ISAPP 2020.
  • —Does not contain a postbiotic. (Different category entirely.)
