00
Position

One sentence.
Built like a wedge.

"Pheeew is the relief brand for the people whose bodies lock up."
The position. Don't drift.

Not a wellness brand. Not a supplement brand. Not a fun-soda brand. A relief brand.

That word — relief — is the entire moat. Every line of copy, every design choice, every customer interaction either delivers relief or doesn't belong.

01
The Wedge

The third lane.
Already empty. Ours.

Clinical gut health is dead (Bloom audit, Seed friction backlash, omega-3 reversal). Fun gut health is now also commodity (Poppi sold to Pepsi, Lemme has Kourtney, Olipop is on Walmart shelves). The lane in between is empty. Sit there.

Lane 1
Clinical
"We optimize your microbiome."
Bloom · AG1 · Seed · Ritual · Pendulum capsules

Status: Dead. Audit-resistant.
Lane 2
Fun
"Gut health is delicious!"
Poppi · Olipop · Lemme · Lifeway · Magic Spoon

Status: Saturated. Performative.
Lane 3 · ours
Relief
"The day your body locks up — and the day it doesn't."
Pheeew (alone)

Status: Open. Ours to lose.

Cool ≠ Fun. Studio Mojave aesthetic already enforces this — Stüssy, Apartamento, Joshua Tree, "LIVIN' EXTRA" diner are not cute. They're confident. Cool girl is unbothered, dry, doesn't perform. The brand's emotional default is calm satisfaction, not joy. Joy is what the user gets. We sell relief.

Relief is what happens
after you stop trying
to convince yourself you're fine.
Why we're in this lane.
02
Posture

Cool girl,
unbothered.

The brand book locked "bratty, confident cool girl mood." This is what that means in practice — not a vibe, an operating standard.

✓ Permitted
  • Names things bluntly. Toilet. Period. Bloat. Locked up. Going.
  • Refuses to soften. No qualifying, no "🤍 sending love" closers.
  • Confident shorthand. Single-word captions. Single-line headlines.
  • Dry irony. Not jokes — irony. Different.
  • Aesthetic self-trust. Doesn't ask whether you like it.
  • Doesn't explain itself. A pour shot doesn't need a caption.
✗ Forbidden
  • Asking permission. "Hey besties, hope this is okay…"
  • Performing positivity. "OMG SO EXCITED!"
  • Hashtag stacking. #guthealth #fitness #wellness #microbiome
  • Pleading for engagement. "Comment below if this is you!"
  • Softening with emoji. No 🌸✨🥰💕🤍 in body copy.
  • "Self-care" platitudes. "You deserve rest." Throw-pillow tier.
  • "Girl gang" / "girlies" / "babes." Forbidden.
  • Manufactured intimacy. "Real talk." "Spilling the tea."
The cool girl test
Would a 32-year-old Korean designer in Seoul who has never said "girlies" in her life say this out loud?
If no, kill the line. This single test sorts ~90% of copy decisions. Apply before publishing anything — caption, ad, email, press quote.
03
Voice

The codified rules.

Sentence-level mechanics. What goes in. What gets cut. The lexicon — and the banishment list.

✓ Sentence rules
  • Avg length. 6–14 words.
  • Periods do work. Three short sentences > one comma-spliced run-on.
  • First word does work. Lead with the body, the moment, the verb. Never with "We" or "Our."
  • Lowercase or sentence case in body. SCREAMING UPPER reserved for one-line headlines.
  • No exclamation marks. Period.
  • Em-dashes are fine. Semicolons are pretentious.
✓ Person + tense
  • Dawon = first person. Always "I." She is one human, not a "we."
  • Brand = second person. "Built for you when…"
  • "Customer" = "you," never "consumer," "fam," or "babe."
  • Present tense for the body. "Your stomach is locked up."
  • Past tense for discovery. "I tried what was on the shelves. None of it worked."
  • Future tense banned. The product earns the future. Copy doesn't sell it.
✓ Keep — the lexicon
tight · locked up · lightness · free · go · going · went · pants buttoning · period week · the day you finally · toilet session · bathroom · stuck · loose · real · pheeew · relief · oasis · desert · lock · release · finally · finally finally · today · tomorrow morning · 7am · day three · the morning after · slow · quiet · still · settled · breath
✗ Banish — the kill list
synbiotic · clinical-grade · doctor-formulated · postbiotic · microbiome · daily wellness · gut health journey · holistic · functional · optimal · balanced · thriving · glow · vibe · obsessed · besties · girlies · babes · "did you know that 95%…" · spill the tea · real talk · sending love · self-care · you deserve · we love · we believe · our community · gut goals · belly bliss · tummy time

(Banished words are fine in fine-print only — Supplement Facts, FAQ /science page where regulatory language is required.)

04
Visual · Studio Mojave

Modernism + handwriting.

Californian modernist desert × warm urban LA studio sensibility. Modernism is the anchor. Handwriting is the punctuation. Artist-cool, intellectual-cool, bratty cool girl. Studio Mojave.

Spatial logic. Modernism = the structure. Grids hold. Margins breathe. Generous whitespace that feels confident, not empty. Handwriting = the punctuation. One handwritten phrase per layout, not five. The handwriting is Dawon's, not a generic font. It's the voice in human form.

Photography. Sun, dust, peach light, palm shadow, ceramic surfaces, wood floors, real bathrooms. Not studio-flat-lay-with-succulents.

Cream is universal. Page background, packaging, IG post background. Cream is the desert sand. Don't go pure white — too clinical. Cream is the warmth.

✓ Photography permits
  • Bathrooms. Photograph them. Tile, faucet, terracotta floor, peach light through window.
  • Hands and necks. Faces optional. The body is the subject.
  • Pour shots are sacred. Real fruit-saturated color, real drip, real countertop.
  • Desert real or implied. Joshua Tree, sun-bleached parking lot, the corner of a beige rug.
✗ Photography forbids
  • Flat lays with succulents and crystals. Forbidden.
  • Before/after body photos. Forbidden.
  • Ring-light beauty-tutorial setups. Forbidden.
  • DM Serif Display. Retired. Replace with Bebas Neue (display) + Inter (body) + Caveat (handwriting).

Naming System

Every name lives
inside the metaphor.

The Oasis-in-Desert metaphor is infinitely extensible. Each retail launch, each new SKU, each milestone is a "new oasis discovered." It compounds.

SKU naming. Format: Pheeew · [Fruit name] OR Pheeew · [Mood word]. Not "Gut + Bloat" (clinical, symptom-pricing). Not "Wild Berries Synbiotic Stick" (mechanism on front, banished).

06
Customer Code

Who they are.
How we name them.

Not "target audience" — actual humans. Lead with their condition, never their demographic.

Who they are. 26–42, 70%+ female, 30%+ male. Has tried at least 3 gut products that didn't work. Has stood in front of a supplement aisle and read back-labels. Has Googled "why am I bloated" at 11pm. Period bloat, IBS, GLP-1 side effects, post-Korean-BBQ, post-flight, dieting. Korean-Americans, Asian-Americans, second-gen diaspora over-indexed. Has Aesop / The Ordinary energy in their bathroom. Owns at least one Stüssy or Comme des Garçons piece. Listens to podcasts on commute. Trusts founder voices over brand voices. Bullshit-detector calibrated very tightly.

✗ Don't call them
customers (in marketing) · consumers · fam · babes · girlies · honeys · community (over-used) · tribe · "you guys"
✓ Do call them
"you" (default) · "anyone who's…" · "the people who…" · their condition before their demographic

09
The Kill List

If anyone proposes any of these,
the answer is no without discussion.

Skit-cast of recurring characters. That's the Performer archetype. We're Catalyst.
Short fictional film series. World Builder requires budget we don't have.
Celebrity endorsement until earned organically.
"Wellness day" calendar campaigns (World Sleep Day, Earth Day, Period Awareness Month).
Rebrand into a softer / cuter / more pastel direction.
Pivot to "fun-soda gut health."
"Synbiotic" / "clinical" / "doctor-formulated" on the front of any pack.
Merch line that's just the logo on a tee.
Trend-jacking content campaign (challenges, dances, audios).
Renaming SKU after a symptom ("Gut + Bloat") instead of a moment.
Hiring stylists who've worked on Bloom, AG1, Lemme, Goop, or Daily Harvest.
Putting "All Vital Organics" anywhere customer-facing.
TikTok Shop launch before organic content engine works.
"Drop" hype theatrics (countdown timers, "only 24 hours!"). We're not a sneaker brand.
Wellness-influencer seeding with anyone who has "wellness" / "gut" in their bio handle.
Founders gift-box PR mailer that costs more than $40 to assemble.

11
The Brand Base Test

Five questions.
Run every decision through them.

For any future move — a new SKU, a new retailer, a new hire, a new copy line — answer all five. Any "I don't know" or "kind of" → kill it and reroll.

Question 1
Does it deliver relief?
Vs fun, vs clinical, vs aesthetic-only.
Question 2
Does it sound like a 32-year-old Korean designer in Seoul talks?
The Dawon test.
Question 3
Does it live in the Oasis / Desert / Road / Water lexicon?
Or does it contradict the metaphor.
Question 4
Is it Catalyst-mode or Helper-mode?
If neither, what is it — and is that allowed.
Question 5
Does it inherit our cool-girl unbotheredness — or does it perform?
Performance breaks the brand. Confidence builds it.
