§ 03 · sanity check

Why these numbers.

Every founder asked for something. Here's the version that honors all four asks at once.

  • ✓ Alex stays above 40%. Lands at 40.47% — the founder/CEO floor everyone agreed on for VC-readiness and decision authority.
  • ✓ Justin lands above 20%. Reaches 20.47% — reflecting his $22,000 commitment and the lift he's giving to Alex's refresh.
  • ✓ Jason gets at least 17%. Lands at 17.47% — credit for being the first risk capital in January, plus the $8,250 he's adding now.
  • ✓ Dawon naturally rises to 11.58%. Her 2,880,000 shares are locked from April's 83(b) filing. With less new cash entering the company, her existing slice becomes proportionally bigger — answering part of her dilution concern without anyone having to give anything up.
  • ✓ 10% option pool reserved. Available for future hires, advisors, and performance-based grants to any founder (Dawon, Jason, Justin) on KPI milestones.
  • ✓ Alex puts in zero new cash. Operating capital comes from Justin and Jason; Alex's refresh is fully funded through their gifts.
  • ✓ No gift tax filings. Both gifts to Alex stay below the $19,000 IRS annual exclusion. No Form 709, no tax events, no external paperwork.
  • ✓ $50,000 in the company. Pheeew now has $50,000 in real operating capital — enough to start formulation, brand work, and Phase 1 manufacturing prep.
A note on Dawon's 11.58%

This isn't a special grant or favor — it's pure math. Dawon's 2,880,000 shares were locked back in April. The total cap table is smaller this round because less new cash is going in, so her fixed shares occupy a slightly bigger slice. The optionality for her to earn more equity through performance milestones (via the 10% pool) is still open. The same path is available to Justin and Jason.
