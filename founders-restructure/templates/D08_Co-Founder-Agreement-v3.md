This Amended and Restated Co-Founder Agreement (this "**Agreement**") is made and entered into as of **[DATE]** (the "**Effective Date**"), by and among:

- **Pheeew, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation (the "**Company**");
- **Seungjoon (Alex) Kim**, an individual ("**Alex**");

## 3. ROLES

Each Founder shall serve in the role described below. Detailed role-based responsibilities (the "**R&R Framework**") are documented at the Company's internal team page (https://allvitalorganics.github.io/pheeew-live/team/) and incorporated by reference. The R&R Framework is open-ended, role-based, and not limited to the specific projects or initiatives existing at the Effective Date.

| Founder | Title | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| **Alex** | Chief Executive Officer (CEO) | Strategy, vision, brand voice, fundraising, vendor relationships, hiring, governance. **Founder safety net** — proactively assists every domain when help is needed. |
| **Jason** | Chief Operating Officer (COO) | Operations, finance, legal, supply chain, 3PL, tech stack, compliance. "Back of house." |
| **Justin** | Chief Growth Officer (CGO) | Partnerships, influencer/creator sourcing, retail buyer outreach, investor sourcing, marketing, biz dev. "Front of house." |
| **Dawon** | Chief Brand Officer (CBO) | Brand identity, voice, design system, content production, customer experience, brand consistency oversight. "Brand owner." |

---

## 4. COLLABORATION PRINCIPLE

The Founders agree to operate under the following principle, which is fundamental to the bootstrap phase of the Company (Months 1–24 from the Effective Date):

> *"Roles and responsibilities define ownership, not boundaries. In the bootstrap phase, every Founder is expected to step into another Founder's domain when help is needed. CEO Alex assists proactively across all domains as a baseline; the rest assist as needed when called. R&R is the framework for accountability — it is not a limit on contribution. No Founder hides behind their lane while another struggles."*

This principle does not modify legal authority (which remains as set forth in the Bylaws) but governs the operational expectations among the Founders.

## 6. RIGHT OF FIRST REFUSAL

In the event any Founder proposes to transfer all or any portion of his or her vested shares of Common Stock to any third party, the Company shall have a right of first refusal to purchase such shares on the terms proposed by the third party. If the Company does not exercise its right of first refusal in full, the other Founders shall have a secondary right of first refusal, pro rata to their respective holdings.

---

## 7. DRAG-ALONG

In the event of a proposed sale of the Company approved by Founders holding at least seventy-five percent (75%) of the outstanding Common Stock, all Founders agree to vote their shares in favor of such transaction and to participate in such sale on substantially the same terms as the approving Founders.

---

## 8. TAG-ALONG

In the event any Founder proposes to transfer shares representing more than ten percent (10%) of the outstanding Common Stock to a third party (other than via a transfer permitted under Section 6), the other Founders shall have the right to participate in such transfer on a pro rata basis on the same terms as the proposing Founder.

---

## 9. CONFIDENTIALITY

Each Founder shall execute the Company's standard Confidential Information and Invention Assignment Agreement ("**CIIAA**") in connection with this Agreement. Each Founder acknowledges that all confidential information of the Company shall be held in confidence and used only for the benefit of the Company.

---

## 10. NON-COMPETITION

During the period of the Founder's continuous service to the Company and for twelve (12) months thereafter, each Founder agrees not to directly compete with the Company in the daily synbiotic supplement category in the United States market, except as may be expressly agreed in writing by the other Founders.

---
