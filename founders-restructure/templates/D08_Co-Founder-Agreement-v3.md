## ARTICLE 3. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

3.1 **Officer Titles.** Each Founder shall serve in the officer capacity set forth below:

| Founder | Title |
|---|---|
| Alex | Chief Executive Officer (CEO) and Secretary |
| Jason | Chief Operating Officer (COO) |
| Justin | Chief Growth Officer (CGO) |
| Dawon | Chief Brand Officer (CBO) |

3.2 **Scope of Responsibilities.** Detailed role-based responsibilities (the "**R&R Framework**") are documented at the Company's internal team page and are incorporated herein by reference. The R&R Framework is open-ended and role-based, and is not limited to the specific projects or initiatives existing as of the Effective Date.

3.3 **Collaboration Principle.** During the bootstrap phase of the Company (defined as the period from the Effective Date through and including the twenty-fourth (24th) calendar month following the Effective Date), the Founders agree to operate under the following principle:

> *Roles and responsibilities define ownership, not boundaries. Each Founder is expected to step into another Founder's domain when help is needed. The Chief Executive Officer assists proactively across all domains as a baseline; the other Founders assist as needed when called upon. The R&R Framework is the framework for accountability; it is not a limit on contribution.*

This Section 3.3 governs the operational expectations among the Founders and does not modify the legal authority of any officer or director, which remains as set forth in the Bylaws.

All grants follow the standard four (4) year vesting schedule with a one (1) year cliff: twenty-five percent (25%) of the shares vest on the one-year anniversary of the applicable Vesting Commencement Date, and the remaining seventy-five percent (75%) vests in equal monthly installments over the following thirty-six (36) months.

## ARTICLE 5. RIGHT OF FIRST REFUSAL

5.1 **Company ROFR.** In the event any Founder proposes to transfer all or any portion of his or her vested shares of Common Stock to any third party, the Company shall have a right of first refusal to purchase such shares on the same terms and conditions proposed by the third party.

5.2 **Founder Secondary ROFR.** If the Company does not exercise its right of first refusal in full within thirty (30) days after receipt of the transfer notice, the other Founders shall have a secondary right of first refusal, exercisable pro rata to their respective holdings of Common Stock, on the same terms and conditions.

5.3 **Permitted Transfers.** The right of first refusal in this Article 5 shall not apply to transfers to a Founder's spouse, lineal descendants, or a trust for the benefit of any of the foregoing for estate planning purposes, provided that the transferee agrees in writing to be bound by this Agreement.

---

## ARTICLE 6. DRAG-ALONG

6.1 In the event of a proposed sale of the Company approved by Founders holding at least seventy-five percent (75%) of the outstanding Common Stock held by the Founders, all Founders agree to vote their shares in favor of such transaction and to participate in such sale on substantially the same terms as the approving Founders.

---

## ARTICLE 7. TAG-ALONG

7.1 In the event any Founder proposes to transfer shares representing more than ten percent (10%) of the outstanding Common Stock to a third party (other than via a transfer permitted under Section 5.3), the other Founders shall have the right to participate in such transfer on a pro rata basis on the same terms and conditions as the proposing Founder.

---

## ARTICLE 8. CONFIDENTIALITY AND IP ASSIGNMENT

8.1 **CIIAA.** Each Founder shall execute a Confidential Information and Invention Assignment Agreement (the "**CIIAA**") with the Company in connection with this Agreement.

8.2 **Confidentiality.** Each Founder acknowledges that all confidential information of the Company shall be held in confidence and used solely for the benefit of the Company.

---

## ARTICLE 9. NON-COMPETITION AND NON-SOLICITATION

9.1 **Non-Competition.** During the period of the Founder's continuous service to the Company and for twelve (12) months thereafter, each Founder agrees not to directly engage in or provide services to any business that competes with the Company in the daily synbiotic supplement category in the United States market, except as may be expressly agreed in writing by the other Founders. The restriction in this Section 9.1 shall be limited to the maximum extent permitted by applicable law and shall not apply where prohibited by the laws of the Founder's primary place of work, including without limitation the laws of the State of California.

9.2 **Non-Solicitation.** During the period of the Founder's continuous service to the Company and for twelve (12) months thereafter, each Founder agrees not to solicit any employee, contractor, or customer of the Company for any competing purpose.

---

## ARTICLE 10. GOVERNANCE

10.1 **Board of Directors.** The Board of Directors of the Company shall consist of one (1) member, Alex, in his capacity as Sole Director, until expanded by future investor agreement or unanimous Founder consent.

10.2 **Officers.** The officers of the Company are as set forth in Section 3.1.

---

## ARTICLE 11. AMENDMENT

11.1 This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by the Company and Founders holding at least seventy-five percent (75%) of the outstanding Common Stock held by the Founders.

---

## ARTICLE 12. SUCCESSION

12.1 This Agreement supersedes and replaces in its entirety the Co-Founder Agreement v2 dated April 13, 2026.
