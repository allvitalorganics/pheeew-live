Copyright takedown.

Effective May 15, 2026 · Pheeew, Inc., a Delaware C-Corporation

Pheeew respects the intellectual property rights of others and asks that visitors and content contributors do the same. This page explains how to submit a DMCA takedown notice.

1. Filing a takedown notice

If you believe content on pheeew.com infringes a copyright you own or control, send a written notice to our designated DMCA agent that includes all of the following:

  1. A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or authorized agent.
  2. Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to be infringed.
  3. Identification of the material claimed to be infringing, with enough detail for us to locate it (a URL is usually sufficient).
  4. Your contact information: name, address, phone, email.
  5. A statement that you have a good-faith belief that the use is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  6. A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information in the notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on its behalf.

3. Counter-notice

If your content was removed in response to a DMCA notice and you believe it was removed in error, you may send a counter-notice that includes:

  1. Your physical or electronic signature.
  2. Identification of the material that was removed and where it appeared before removal.
  3. A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good-faith belief the material was removed by mistake or misidentification.
  4. Your name, address, phone, and a statement consenting to jurisdiction in the federal district court for the address provided.

4. Repeat infringers

Pheeew terminates accounts of users determined to be repeat infringers under appropriate circumstances.

5. Misrepresentations

Under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f), any person who knowingly materially misrepresents that material is infringing (or was removed in error) may be liable for damages.
