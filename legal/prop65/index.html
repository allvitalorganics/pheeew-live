◆ California Prop 65

California Proposition 65.

Effective May 15, 2026 · Pheeew, Inc., a Delaware C-Corporation

This notice is required under California's Safe Drinking Water and Toxic Enforcement Act of 1986 ("Proposition 65" or "Prop 65").

1. About Prop 65

Proposition 65 requires California businesses to provide warnings about significant exposure to chemicals that cause cancer, birth defects, or other reproductive harm. The official list of regulated chemicals is maintained by California's Office of Environmental Health Hazard Assessment (OEHHA) and is updated at least annually.

2. Pheeew & Prop 65

Pheeew's ingredients (real fruit puree, prebiotic fibers, probiotic culture, natural acidifier) are food-grade and produced in a FDA-registered, HACCP-certified, GMP facility. Based on the formulation, the manufacturing process, and the suppliers' Certificates of Analysis, we do not expect Pheeew to trigger a Prop 65 warning under current OEHHA listings and exposure thresholds.

That said, certain foods — including produce — naturally contain trace amounts of substances on the Prop 65 list (such as lead in soil-grown fruit) below regulatory action levels. If new testing data, supplier disclosures, or OEHHA updates change this assessment, we will update this page and add the required warning to the affected pouches.

3. How to check the current status

For the most current information on a specific Pheeew SKU, email help@pheeew.com with the SKU and batch number from your pouch.

4. Official resources

  • OEHHA Prop 65 list: oehha.ca.gov
  • Prop 65 warnings guidance: p65warnings.ca.gov
