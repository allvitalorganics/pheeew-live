What it is

Deadline

FDA compliance
Pheeew is a conventional food, not a dietary supplement — the regulatory pathway that lets us sell without 30-day notification
Category
Conventional food (FDA-regulated under FD&C Act). NOT classified as a dietary supplement.
Why it matters
Conventional food = no mandatory disclaimer ("These statements have not been evaluated…"), no 75-day FDA notification for new ingredients, fewer label warnings. Supplement classification triggers all of that.
Probiotic GRAS status
Bacillus coagulans is GRAS-affirmed (Generally Recognized As Safe) for conventional food use. 1B CFU per pouch is well under any safety concern threshold.
Nutrition Facts
FDA-compliant Nutrition Facts panel required. Includes: serving size (80mL), calories, sugars, fiber (10g — disclosed), and required micronutrients.
Allergen labeling
Big 9 allergens (milk, eggs, fish, shellfish, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat, soy, sesame). Pheeew's puree base doesn't contain any of the top 9 — declare "May contain" only if there's cross-contamination risk in the facility.
Country of origin
"Product of Korea" · "Distributed by Pheeew, Inc., Los Angeles CA" on every pouch.
Claims we CAN'T make
"Cures," "treats," "prevents" any condition (drug claims). "Boosts immunity," "supports digestion" require Structure/Function claim substantiation and disclaimers. Default: stay descriptive — what's in it, how it tastes, when you'd drink it.
Claims we CAN make
Nutrient content claims ("good source of fiber" at >10% DV — we qualify at 10g). Ingredient descriptions. "Synbiotic" is OK as long as we describe what it means.
Facility registration
Pheeew, Inc. doesn't manufacture — 좋은영농 does. Importer of record registers with FDA as a food facility under Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA). Annual.
FSVP
Foreign Supplier Verification Program required for imported food. Pheeew, Inc. is the importer → Jason owns the FSVP documentation including supplier audits.
FTC compliance · advertising & testimonials
The agency that polices what we say in ads, on socials, and in influencer posts
Substantiation rule
Every claim about Pheeew (energy, gut health, regularity, fullness, hydration) must have a reasonable basis on file before we make the claim. Build a Claims Substantiation Doc: claim → supporting study/citation → date. Update as we add claims.
Testimonials
Customer reviews must reflect typical experience. If we highlight a non-typical result ("lost 10 lbs"), we must disclose it's not typical. Default: never feature aspirational outcomes.
Influencer disclosure (2023 update)
"#ad" or "Paid partnership" must be clear and prominent in caption. Hidden in 30 hashtags = non-compliant. Story disclosures must persist on screen for the duration of the brand mention.
Endorsement Guides
Material connection (free product, paid posts) must be disclosed. Even free product to a creator triggers disclosure — creators must say "PR" or "gifted" or "#ad". Build this into every creator outreach template.
Negative-option marketing
Subscription auto-renewal must have: clear upfront disclosure, easy cancellation (single click in customer portal), reminder before renewal. Recharge handles most of this; we audit annually.
Made in USA
We don't make this claim — product is made in Korea. Avoid any language that implies US manufacture.
Comparative claims
"Better than [competitor]" requires head-to-head substantiation. Default: don't name competitors. Talk about the category.

Positioning · long form
The paragraph version for site copy, deck, vendor intros

Pheeew is a smoothie for your gut. An 80mL synbiotic puree pouch — real fruit blended with 10g of prebiotic fiber (XOS + Fibersol-2) and 1B CFU of Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969 — for people who tried doing more for their gut, gave up, and want something they'll actually finish. We're not a juice. We're not a shot. We're not a supplement. We're the food version of the gut routine.

The functional ingredients
Probiotic · 1B CFU per pouch
Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969®
Sanzyme Biologics, India. Spore-form, heat-stable to 180°F — enables hot-fill in the pouch. 1 billion CFU per 80mL serve · stable through 18-month ambient shelf life. 30+ clinical studies including IBS RCT, constipation RCT, and NK-cell immunomodulatory trials. GRAS-affirmed for use in conventional food.
Prebiotic · 10g per pouch
XOS + Fibersol-2 blend
XOS (xylo-oligosaccharide) is a short-chain prebiotic fermented selectively by Bifidobacteria. Fibersol-2® (Matsutani/ADM) is a clinically-studied soluble resistant maltodextrin that adds bulk + supports regularity. Combined at 10g/pouch — roughly 30–40% of the FDA's daily fiber recommendation in a single 80mL serve.
Synbiotic = prebiotic + probiotic. Never "postbiotic" — that's a different category.
