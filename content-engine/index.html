01 · Identity

Brand identity

One paragraph. This is the anchor — every line of voice ultimately reports back here.

Pheeew is a gut-health brand built around a single hero format — an 80mL puree pouch you can finish in 30 seconds. We started here because every gut routine we tried failed for the same reason: it was too much work. Capsules needed an empty stomach. Powders needed a blender. The $14 bottles needed a fridge. So we kept the dose — 10g of prebiotic fiber, 1 billion probiotic CFU, real fruit puree — and removed everything else.

A smoothie for your gut.

Architecture: Gut + (function). Phase 1 SKU is Mojave Sunrise (tropical fruit · mango · banana). Made in Korea, distributed from LA. Pre-launch, late Aug–Sep 2026 target.

02 · Audience

Who we're talking to

Late-20s to mid-40s. Health-curious but burned by previous routines that didn't stick. Reads. Cooks sometimes. Knows their morning is the only daily window they can actually defend. Skeptical of biohacker culture and of yoga-girl wellness. Wants substance + practicality.

The daily gut routine that actually sticks. Not the most science. Not the most natural. The one you finish.

04 · Voice

Voice traits

Tone register

  • Warm but specific. Not soft. Not corporate. Like a knowledgeable friend.
  • Declarative, not preachy. "Your gut bacteria recognize 30 plants by Friday." not "Did you know that your gut bacteria…"
  • Curious, not certain. When science is incomplete, say so. "The connection isn't debated — the cause is."
  • A little dry humor allowed. Never punchy or sassy. Olipop's energy is too loud for us.

Vocabulary

Plain English first. Use the scientific term, then translate. Or skip the term entirely.

"Your gut makes 90% of your body's serotonin — the mood chemical."
"Enteroendocrine cells synthesize 5-hydroxytryptamine via tryptophan hydroxylase…"

Korean phrases are welcome when they earn their place — usually for cultural texture, never as decoration.

"Korean grandmothers had a word for it before the science did: 속이 편하다."
"Try our healthy 100% 천연 ingredient pouch!"

No supplement-industry words. Avoid: stack, hack, optimize, biohack, dosage, regimen, protocol.

No wellness-industry words. Avoid: glow, radiance, journey, sacred, intention, "rituals" (as a noun · feels precious).

Sentence rhythm

  • Mix short. Medium. Then occasionally one longer sentence packed with a real observation.
  • Avoid same-length sentences in sequence. That's where AI voice creeps in.
  • Comma splices are allowed if they land. "She tried everything, gave up three times, then started looking at the problem differently."

Anchor sentences (temperature dial · use these as North Stars)

05 · Anti-patterns

What not to do

The rules that compound. Hit any one of these and the brand is off-temperature.

Hard No

Medical claims. Never "treats / cures / prevents / heals." FDA / FTC line.
Fake authorship. Don't invent writer names or quotes. If asked: "AI-assisted editorial."
Fabricated science. Every research reference verifies to a primary source. If you can't verify, flag [TBD].
"Juice" / "shot" / "drink in bottle." It's a puree pouch. Period.
"Postbiotic." Separate category from synbiotic. Don't conflate.
"MOHO" reference. The old studio name is retired. Appears nowhere.
"@drinkpheeew." Correct handle is @saypheeew only.
"Distributed by Pheeew, Inc." on social. Legal boilerplate stays on packaging.
Other portfolio brands. Hard separation — no mention of any other brand in Pheeew content.
Specific LA neighborhood detail. Public language is "LA + Korea." Neighborhoods stay internal.
"Pronounceable ingredients" + scientific names in same post. Internally inconsistent.

Soft Avoid

Exclamation marks. Never as enthusiasm. Maybe in dialogue.
Emojis as filler. Max one per post as visual anchor — never decorative.
Startup-language. "Disrupting…" / "rethinking…" / "the future of gut." All cringe.
Wellness-jargon. "Self-care Sunday," "sip-and-savor," "mindful morning ritual." Skip.
Bro-science. "Stack THIS for your microbiome 🔥💪." Skip.
Listicle openers. "5 ways to…" — clickbait. Lead with the insight instead.

P1 · Microbiome 101
Your gut bacteria recognize 30 different plants by Friday.
Most Americans hit 7. The American Gut Project — 11,336 people, the largest microbiome dataset in the world — found a clean correlation: people who eat 30+ different plant species per week have measurably more diverse microbiomes than people stuck under 10.
"Different" doesn't mean exotic. Coffee is a plant. Cinnamon is a plant. Olive oil is a plant. A handful of mixed berries is 4 plants if it has 4 colors.
Count your plants this week. Aim for 15. Tell us your number.

Source
American Gut Project (UCSD · McDonald et al., 2018 · mSystems)

P2 · The Fiber Gap
Fiber isn't a supplement. It's a missing meal.
The FDA says adults need 25–38g of fiber daily. The average American gets 15. That's not a vitamin gap — that's not eating a meal's worth of plants.
One Mojave Sunrise pouch carries 10g. So if you hit 15 from the rest of your day, you're at 25 — the lower bound of "enough." Probably for the first time in years.
What's your fiber count today? Honest answers only.
Visual
Photo. Half-eaten avocado, chia seeds in a spoon, single pouch on wooden counter. Morning light.
Source
USDA Dietary Guidelines 2020–2025 · Quagliani & Felt-Gunderson 2017 (Am J Lifestyle Med)

P3 · Gut · Brain · Body
Anxiety has a gut signature.
UCLA researchers found that people with persistent low-grade anxiety have measurably different gut bacteria — lower diversity, fewer butyrate-producers — than people who don't. Cause or effect is still debated. The connection isn't.
Your gut makes about 90% of your body's serotonin. Not your brain.
How does your gut feel when you're stressed? Tell us — we read every comment.

10 · Citations

Research citation discipline

If a post references a study:

  1. Name the source (university, journal, year) inline · short.
  2. Don't fabricate. If you can't verify, flag [TBD] in the caption · Alex/Dawon checks before publish.
  3. Verified study seed list · use confidently:
SourceWhat it covers
American Gut Project (UCSD)Plant diversity → microbiome diversity
Mayer et al., UCLAGut-brain axis · anxiety correlation
Sonnenburg lab (Stanford)Fiber → microbiome diversity
Cell · Nature Medicine · GastroenterologyTop-tier journals to draw from
Yano et al., Cell 2015Gut bacteria regulate serotonin biosynthesis
Thaiss et al., Cell 2014Microbiome circadian rhythm

Open lane: any PubMed / journal DOI verified by Alex before posting. Don't pull from Twitter / TikTok / blog summaries without going to primary.
