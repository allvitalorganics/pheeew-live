Honest answers

What people ask.

Probiotic, prebiotic — what's the difference?−

A probiotic is a live bacterium you're adding. A prebiotic is the fiber that feeds the bacteria already living in you. Pheeew is both — that's what "synbiotic" means. The fiber feeds the colony. The spore is the new arrival.

Why "spore-form" matters.−

Most probiotics die in stomach acid before they ever reach the gut. Spore-form Bacillus coagulans stays asleep through the stomach — sealed in its protective coat — then wakes up in the small intestine. That's the difference between a probiotic that survives and one that just looks impressive on a label.

How long until I notice it?−

Most patterns show in 3–7 days for bloat. Bowel rhythm benefits arrive around day 7–14. Microbiome composition changes take 30+ days. Your body decides the pace.

Will I get bloated at first?−

Sometimes, for 2–3 days, especially if your fiber baseline is low. Halve the dose for the first week if it's intense, then build up.

Why three ingredients? Why not more?−

Because every additional ingredient has to earn its weight. Three at full dose beats five at half-dose. Most synbiotics throw in extras to fill out a label — we'd rather you read every line and trust each one.
