Pricing across 4 channels

One manufactured SKU. Multiple price tiers.

Naju produces ONE thing — a 15-pouch box. Every channel ships multiples. This protects MAP discipline from day 1 — sub price ($2.63/pouch) is the brand floor, retail SRP ($3.33/pouch) is the ceiling.

MAP Policy · day 1
No partner advertises Pheeew · 15-box below $44.99 except in pre-approved promotional windows.

Legally enforceable in agreements with Amazon, retailers, distributors. Without MAP, DTC sub gets undercut by Amazon resellers within 6 months and the brand pricing collapses.

Sub savings logic
Sub saves $0.40–0.70/pouch vs. one-time/retail.

Trial $3.00 → Sub Core $2.63 = 12% savings. Trial $3.00 → Sub Committed $2.32 = 23% savings. Strong subscription incentive without breaking unit economics.

08
Risk register

5 risks · ranked.

What kills this in order of likelihood. The top 2 are the launch-binding constraints — everything else is manageable.

01
CAC overshoots $40 target
Most likely failure mode. Meta cold traffic typically $80–150 for DTC supplements. If we exceed $40, unit econ breaks. Mitigation: organic-led launch (Founders' List + Dawon's @saypheeew + influencer seeding); avoid Meta cold traffic until product-market fit signal clear.
High likelihood
High impact
02
F&F SAFE closes slower than expected
If only $15K of $30K target lands by mid-June, we hit the Naju deposit but lack the Sep marketing buffer. Mitigation: Founders' List pre-orders generate non-dilutive cash earlier; lean harder on that channel; reduce marketing spend in first 90 days.
Medium likelihood
High impact
03
Sub retention <4 months
If avg retention drops to 3 months, LTV halves. If 2 months, model breaks. Mitigation: day-3 / day-7 / day-30 email flow with founder voice; "pause-don't-cancel" option in dashboard; quarterly reformulation cadence to keep product fresh.
Medium likelihood
High impact
04
Naju production delay (4-8 weeks)
Pushes launch from September to October-November. Founders' List refunds if delay is communicated badly. Mitigation: weekly Naju standup, transparent comms to Founders' List from day 1, build a 4-week buffer into the September date if possible.
Low likelihood
Medium impact
05
Amazon listing rejected / category competition
Phase 2 risk. Premium gut-health supplement category on Amazon is crowded. SEO + reviews matter. Mitigation: launch Amazon at month 6 with ≥30 5-star reviews from DTC customers, optimized listing copy, branded keyword campaigns.
Low likelihood
Medium impact
