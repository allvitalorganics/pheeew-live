The product

What is Pheeew?

Pheeew is an 80mL synbiotic puree pouch — a daily gut habit you actually finish. Each pouch has 10g of prebiotic fiber (XOS + Fibersol-2), 1 billion CFU of Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969 (a spore-form probiotic), and real fruit. No added sugar, no preservatives, no synthetic flavors. Shelf-stable at room temperature for 18 months. Drink one a day.

Why a pouch instead of a pill, powder, or bottle?

We tested all of them. Capsules feel like a supplement (and require an empty stomach for some strains). Powders turn breakfast into a science experiment. Bottles cost more, take fridge space, and signal "I'm being healthy" in a way that doesn't survive month two of a real routine. The pouch is one sip, room temperature, anywhere — your bag, your desk, your fridge. It survives the habit.

Is Pheeew a supplement or a food?

A conventional food. Not a dietary supplement. The probiotic strain we use (Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969) is GRAS-affirmed for use in conventional food, so we can deliver it without using the supplement category. This means no mandatory disclaimer label, no 75-day FDA pre-notification, and no "supplement aisle" placement when we hit retail. It also means we don't make health or disease claims — Pheeew is food that happens to be good for your gut.

What does it taste like?

Mojave Sunrise — our Phase 1 flavor — is tropical fruit, mango, and banana. Sweet enough to feel like a treat, light enough to feel like food, not a dessert. Cold tastes like a slushy. Frozen tastes like a popsicle. Room temperature tastes like a smoothie. There's no added sugar — the sweetness comes from the real fruit puree.

When are the other flavors coming?

Phase 2 (berry-forward) and Phase 3 (ginger & pear) are on the roadmap. Order TBD — we'll pace based on what the audience tells us they actually want after Mojave Sunrise lands. Tell us your preference on the contact page and it'll factor in.

The ingredients

What's the fiber blend?

10g of combined prebiotic fiber per pouch. Half is XOS (xylo-oligosaccharide) — a short-chain prebiotic that selectively feeds Bifidobacteria. The other half is Fibersol-2® — a clinically-studied resistant maltodextrin from Matsutani/ADM that adds bulk and supports regularity. Both are well-tolerated at the doses we use.

What's the probiotic, and what's "1B CFU" mean?

Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969® from Sanzyme Biologics. Spore-form (it protects itself with a shell) so it survives stomach acid, bile, and the heat of manufacturing. CFU = "colony forming units" — the count of living bacteria. 1B means one billion live bacteria per pouch on the day you drink it, all the way through the 18-month shelf life.

Is there added sugar?

No added sugar. The sweetness in Mojave Sunrise comes entirely from the real fruit puree (tropical fruit, mango, banana). No cane sugar, no high-fructose corn syrup, no agave. No synthetic sweeteners either — no sucralose, no aspartame, no stevia. The Nutrition Facts panel discloses the naturally occurring sugars from the fruit.

Allergens?

No top-9 allergens (milk, eggs, fish, shellfish, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat, soy, sesame) in Mojave Sunrise. Produced in an allergen-free production line in a facility that follows HACCP and GMP food safety standards.

Vegan? Gluten-free?

Yes to both. No animal products. No gluten. No GMO ingredients.

Where is it made?

Manufactured in a FDA-registered, HACCP-certified, GMP food facility in South Korea. Distributed by Pheeew, Inc. (Los Angeles, Delaware C-Corporation). Country of origin disclosed on every pouch: "Product of Korea · Distributed by Pheeew, Inc., Los Angeles CA."

How to use it

When should I drink it?

Anytime. Most people pick a consistent time — usually morning — because consistency builds the habit faster than time-of-day optimization. With breakfast, with coffee, before lunch, after a workout. The probiotic doesn't require an empty stomach (it's spore-form, it survives anything).

How long until I feel something?

Most people notice their gut waking up in week 1 — more frequent, more comfortable bowel movements. Some initial fiber adjustment is normal. By weeks 2–3 the rhythm starts settling. Weeks 4–6 are when the probiotic population is well-established and downstream effects (skin, energy, mood) sometimes follow. By week 8 the pouch should feel like part of your routine, not a project.

Does it need refrigeration?

No. The hot-fill process and spore-form probiotic mean Pheeew is shelf-stable at room temperature for 18 months. Refrigerating it makes it taste like a slushy. Freezing turns it into a popsicle. All three serving temperatures work for your gut.

Can I take it with my other supplements / medications?

Pheeew is a food, not a supplement — there are no known interactions of concern for the ingredients at the doses we use. That said, we're not your doctor. If you're on a specific medication regimen, an autoimmune protocol, or you're pregnant or breastfeeding, check with your healthcare provider before starting anything new.

Can kids drink it?

Pheeew is formulated for adults. Children under 13 should only consume it with a parent's call (and ideally a pediatrician's input). We don't market to kids and don't recommend it as a routine for kids without that conversation.
