The product

Everyday questions.

What it is, how to take it, and what to expect.

What is Pheeew?

A daily gut-support stick powder. Tear a stick, stir into anything you drink. 14g of fiber (12g Fibersol-2L + 2g XOS), 2 billion CFU of Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969 (a spore-form probiotic), and a trace of silica as an anti-caking agent. That's it.

Why unflavored?

Two reasons. First, flavor masking is the #1 way gut products underdose — they need sweetener to cover up the taste of real fiber. Skipping flavor lets us push the dose. Second, you already drink something in the morning. We'd rather pour into that than ask you to swap it.

How do I take it?

One stick a day. Tear at the notch, pour into anything liquid (coffee, oat milk, smoothie, water, juice — it's all fine), stir 10 seconds. Drink. Hot or cold both work — the spore probiotic is heat-stable.

Will I taste anything?

Almost nothing. There's a very slight thickening from the fiber if you use a thin liquid like water. In milk, oat milk, smoothies, or coffee — you won't notice it.

When will I see results?

Most fiber and probiotic clinical work measures outcomes between weeks 4 and 8. Many people notice regularity changes in the first 1–2 weeks. Microbiomes vary — we don't promise specific timelines, but the trial 14-pack exists for a reason.

What if it doesn't work for me?

30-day refund. No questions, no return required. Email hello@pheeew.com and we'll make it right.

Can I take more than one stick a day?

You can — the formula is well-tolerated at multiple servings. We'd suggest ramping up: one stick a day for the first week, then add a second if you want. Your gut microbiome adapts; sudden fiber increases can cause bloating in the first few days.

What's a "spore-form" probiotic?

A probiotic strain that survives in a dormant spore state on the shelf, then activates in the gut. Unlike most probiotics that need refrigeration and barely survive stomach acid, spore-form probiotics like SNZ 1969 are stable at room temperature and reach the small intestine alive. Strain rigor →

Is SNZ 1969 the same as LactoSpore or GanedenBC30?

No. All three are Bacillus coagulans, but they're different strains from different suppliers — LactoSpore is MTCC 5856 (Sami-Sabinsa), GanedenBC30 is GBI-30 6086 (Kerry), and SNZ 1969 is from Sanzyme. Each is studied separately, and we don't claim cross-strain equivalence.

Is 14g of fiber a lot?

It's about 56% of the US daily recommendation (25g for adult women, 38g for adult men). You'd still want to get fiber from food. Pheeew is meant as a daily floor, not a replacement for vegetables.

Is it safe with my medication?

Pheeew is food-grade fiber + a GRAS probiotic. We're not aware of meaningful drug interactions. If you're on prescription medication — especially anything where timing matters — please run it by your doctor before adding any daily supplement.

Vegan, gluten-free, allergens?

Vegan. Gluten-free. Dairy-free. No soy, no nuts, no major allergens. Manufactured in a GMP facility that handles other powders — full allergen statement on the box.

Is it a synbiotic?

Yes — specifically a complementary synbiotic per the 2020 ISAPP consensus statement. Each component (prebiotic + probiotic) was chosen on its own merits. We don't claim it's a synergistic synbiotic — that would require evidence that the prebiotic preferentially feeds this probiotic, and we're not making that claim.
