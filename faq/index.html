The product

The product, in plain English.

What is Pheeew?

A daily synbiotic stick. One stick contains 10g of prebiotic fiber (8g Fibersol-2L soluble corn fiber + 2g xylo-oligosaccharide) and 1 billion live spore cultures of Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969. Three core ingredients, no filler.

What is a synbiotic?

A synbiotic is a prebiotic and a probiotic combined in one product. The prebiotic is fiber that selectively feeds beneficial gut bacteria. The probiotic is a live bacterial strain.

Pheeew is a complementary synbiotic per the ISAPP 2020 consensus — both ingredients earn their evidence independently before they're combined.

How do I take it?

One stick a day. Tear at the notch, drink straight or stir into a few ounces of cold liquid. Same time every day works best — most people pair it with morning coffee or a glass of water.

Will I taste anything?

The flavor is light peach. The peach purée carries it on its own — no stevia, monk fruit, or sucralose. There's a slight thickness from the fiber if you stir into water; in coffee or smoothies you barely notice.

How long until I notice it?

Most patterns show in 3 to 7 days for bloat. Bowel regularity benefits arrive around day 7 to 14. Microbiome composition shifts take 30+ days.

We can't promise a specific outcome (the FDA wouldn't let us, and your body is its own thing), but the pattern in our customer reviews is consistent.

What if it doesn't work for me?

Can I take more than one stick a day?

You can — the formula is well-tolerated at multiple servings. We'd suggest ramping up: one stick a day for the first week, then add a second if you want. Sudden fiber increases can cause temporary bloating.

The formula

About the three.

Why these three ingredients?

Each earns its weight. Fibersol-2L is the most-studied resistant maltodextrin in the literature. XOS is bifidogenic at low dose. B. coagulans SNZ 1969 survives stomach acid as a spore. Three at full dose beats five at half-dose.

Why spore-form probiotic?

Most probiotics die in stomach acid (pH 1.5) before reaching the gut. Spore-form B. coagulans stays dormant in its protective coat through the stomach, then germinates in the small intestine when bile and pancreatic enzymes signal the wake-up. No refrigeration needed.

Why Fibersol-2L specifically?

Fibersol-2L is a soluble corn fiber made by ADM. It's the most extensively studied resistant maltodextrin in clinical literature — backed by human RCTs for bowel regularity, postprandial glucose response, and SCFA production.

What's XOS doing here?

Xylo-oligosaccharide is a short-chain prebiotic that's bifidogenic at low dose — it selectively feeds Bifidobacterium without producing the gas burden that comes with larger inulin doses.

Why three? Why not more?

Because every additional ingredient has to earn its weight. Most synbiotics throw in five or six things at half-doses to look comprehensive on a label. We chose three at full dose. Restraint is the receipt.

Safety

Safety + sensitivities.

Is it safe during pregnancy or nursing?

All three core ingredients have FDA GRAS coverage at our dose level. That said, please consult your physician before adding any supplement during pregnancy or while nursing. That's not a hedge — it's the right call for any new product entering your routine during that period.

Can I take it with medications?

There are no known interactions between fiber, XOS, or Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969 and most medications. If you're on prescription medication — particularly antibiotics, immunosuppressants, or anticoagulants — please consult your doctor before starting.

Will I feel bloated at first?

Sometimes for 2–3 days, especially if your fiber baseline is low. Soluble corn fiber is the most-tolerated resistant maltodextrin in the literature, but going from 5g to 15g of daily fiber is a real shift. If intense, halve the dose for the first week and build up.

What about allergens?

The active blend contains no Big 9 allergens (milk, eggs, fish, shellfish, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat, soy, sesame). Note: Fibersol-2L is corn-derived. We disclose this voluntarily even though corn is not on the FDA Big 9 list. Always check the most current ingredients panel on your specific lot.

Is it suitable for children?

The formula is well-tolerated in adults. We haven't conducted pediatric-specific testing, so we don't recommend it for children under 18 without consulting a pediatric provider.
