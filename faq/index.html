The product

The product,
in plain English.

What is Pheeew?

A daily synbiotic stick. One stick contains 10g of prebiotic fiber (8g Fibersol-2L soluble corn fiber + 2g xylo-oligosaccharide) and 1 billion live spore cultures of Bacillus coagulans SNZ 1969. Three core ingredients, no filler.

What is a synbiotic?

A prebiotic and a probiotic combined. Prebiotic fiber feeds beneficial gut bacteria. Probiotic strain is the bacteria itself.

Pheeew is a complementary synbiotic per the ISAPP 2020 consensus¹ — both ingredients earn their evidence independently before being combined.

How do I take it?

One stick a day. Tear at the notch, drink straight or stir into a few ounces of cold liquid. Same time daily — most pair with morning coffee or water.

Will I taste anything?

Light peach. The peach purée carries the flavor — no stevia, monk fruit, or sucralose. Slight thickness from fiber if you stir into water; in coffee or smoothies you barely notice.

How long until I notice it?

Most patterns show in 3 to 7 days for bloat. Bowel regularity benefits arrive around day 7 to 14. Microbiome composition shifts take 30+ days.²

We can't promise specific outcomes (the FDA wouldn't let us, and your body is its own thing), but the pattern is consistent.

What if it doesn't work for me?

60-day money-back guarantee. Email hello@pheeew.com within 60 days of your first delivery.

Can I take more than one stick a day?

You can — the formula is well-tolerated at multiple servings. Suggested ramp-up: one stick a day for the first week, then add a second if you want. Sudden fiber increases can cause temporary bloating.

The formula

About the three
ingredients.

Why these three ingredients?

Each earns its weight. Fibersol-2L is the most-studied resistant maltodextrin in clinical literature³. XOS is bifidogenic at low dose⁴. B. coagulans SNZ 1969 survives stomach acid. Three at full dose beats five at half-dose.

Why spore-form?

Most probiotics die in stomach acid (pH 1.5)⁵. Spore-form B. coagulans stays dormant in its protective coat through the stomach, then germinates in the small intestine when bile and pancreatic enzymes signal the wake-up. No refrigeration needed.

Why Fibersol-2L specifically?

Made by ADM. The most extensively studied resistant maltodextrin in clinical literature — backed by human RCTs for bowel regularity, postprandial glucose response, and SCFA production³.

What's XOS doing here?

Xylo-oligosaccharide is a short-chain prebiotic that's bifidogenic at low dose⁴ — selectively feeds Bifidobacterium without producing the gas burden that comes with larger inulin doses.

Why three? Why not more?

Every additional ingredient has to earn its weight. Most synbiotics throw in five or six things at half-doses to look comprehensive. We chose three at full dose. Restraint is the receipt.

Safety

Safety +
sensitivities.

Is it safe during pregnancy or nursing?

All three core ingredients have FDA GRAS coverage at our dose level⁶. Please consult your physician before adding any supplement during pregnancy or nursing. That's the right call for any new product entering your routine during that period.

Can I take it with medications?

No known interactions for most medications. Consult your doctor if you're on antibiotics, immunosuppressants, or anticoagulants.

Will I bloat at first?

Sometimes — for 2–3 days, especially if your fiber baseline is low. Soluble corn fiber is the most-tolerated resistant maltodextrin in the literature³, but going from 5g to 15g of daily fiber is a real shift. If intense, halve the dose for the first week.

What about allergens?

The active blend contains no Big 9 allergens (milk, eggs, fish, shellfish, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat, soy, sesame). Note: Fibersol-2L is corn-derived. We disclose this voluntarily even though corn isn't on the FDA Big 9 list.

Is it suitable for children?

The formula is well-tolerated in adults. We haven't conducted pediatric-specific testing, so we don't recommend it for children under 18 without consulting a pediatric provider.
